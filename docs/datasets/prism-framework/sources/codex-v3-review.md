PRISM v2.1 is directionally strong, but as a distillation scaffold for Qwen 3.5 0.8B it is trying to do too many things at once. The current prompt mixes diagnostic reasoning, preventive medicine, and frontier geroscience in one chain, so the small model will tend to learn mechanistic style and longevity overcall rather than robust clinical discrimination. The best v3 is not a larger prompt; it is a smaller core prompt plus route-specific overlays, audited evidence tables, and tools.

1. **Structural critique**  
The 6-step scaffold is good for a teacher model, but too rigid for a 0.8B student. The compact prompt forces almost everything through the same frame ([PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L24)), even though the full reference promises route-specific behavior ([PRISM-v2-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-v2-system-prompt.md#L25)). In practice, the samples show route leakage: BASIC SCIENCE still gets a faux differential ([test-mechanistic.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/examples/test-mechanistic.md#L102)) and PEDIATRIC just reuses the adult template ([test-batch-b.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/examples/test-batch-b.md#L79)). For v3, compress the default diagnostic path to 4 steps: `route/safety -> problem representation -> top hypotheses with decisive for/against evidence -> answer + uncertainty`; make `lab interpretation` and `clinical implications` conditional, not default.

2. **Mechanistic depth calibration**  
The mechanistic imperative is too aggressive for a universal prompt. Requiring molecular target plus signaling cascade in routine cases ([PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L10)) will teach pathway ornamentation, not necessarily better reasoning. For 0.8B, the sweet spot is one mechanistic layer above pattern recognition: organ/system physiology for most cases, one key pathway for pharmacology or biomarker interpretation, and full cascade tracing only for BASIC SCIENCE/pharmacology. Otherwise the model will learn to spray `DAG/PKCe`, `NLRP3`, `SASP`, `mTORC1` as style tokens, which is still pattern matching in mechanistic clothing.

3. **Missing clinical domains**  
The biggest problem is not that the routes are absent; it is that they are named but not actually implemented. Acute care, pregnancy, pediatrics, psychiatry, geriatrics, and post-op are placeholders in the full prompt ([PRISM-v2-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-v2-system-prompt.md#L25)), but there are no real route-specific reasoning rules for airway/breathing/circulation, shock, acid-base/toxicology, ectopic pregnancy, postpartum issues, pediatric fever/sepsis, weight-based dosing, suicide/violence risk, delirium vs psychosis, geriatric deprescribing, Beers/anticholinergic burden, renal dose adjustment, or procedure/POD-specific surgical complications. Drug-interaction reasoning is also underdeveloped: sparse PGx flags are not enough for real medication reasoning.

4. **Evidence quality and misclassification**  
Several “optimal” ranges and drug claims are overstated or mis-tiered. `Lp(a) <30 [A]`, `eGFR >90 [A]`, `Vitamin D 50-80 [B]`, `TSH 0.5-2.0 [B]`, `ApoB>60` as a statin trigger, and telmisartan as the “premier longevity ARB” are not defensible as currently written ([PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L67), [PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L80), [PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L74), [PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L75), [PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L119), [PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/PRISM-compact-system-prompt.md#L120)). Current guidance supports ApoB `>=130 mg/dL` and Lp(a) `>=50 mg/dL` or `>=125 nmol/L` as risk-enhancing, not ApoB 60 or Lp(a) 30 as Tier A ([ACC/AHA primary prevention](https://www.acc.org/Latest-in-Cardiology/Articles/2019/03/07/16/00/2019-ACC-AHA-Guideline-on-the-Primary-Prevention-of-CV-Disease), [ACC Lp(a) update](https://www.acc.org/Latest-in-Cardiology/Articles/2023/09/19/10/11/An-Update-on-Lipoprotein-a)). KDIGO 2024 explicitly does not equate G1/G2 without kidney damage with CKD, so `<90 = nephron reserve failure` is not Tier A ([KDIGO 2024](https://kdigo.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/KDIGO-2024-CKD-Guideline.pdf)). Endocrine guidance does not support routine vitamin D testing or high prevention targets in healthy adults, and nonpregnant subclinical hypothyroidism is not defined by `TSH >2.5` ([Endocrine Society 2024](https://www.endocrine.org/clinical-practice-guidelines/vitamin-d-for-prevention-of-disease), [ASRM 2024](https://www.asrm.org/practice-guidance/practice-committee-documents/subclinical-hypothyroidism-in-the-infertile-female-population-a-guideline/)).  
The drug table has a deeper structural flaw: evidence is claim-specific, not molecule-specific. GLP-1 RAs are Tier A for obesity/CVD, not for “mTOR-like multi-omic aging signatures”; SGLT2 inhibitors are Tier A for HF/CKD/T2D, not for senolysis; LDN is mixed small-study evidence, not clean Tier B. The cited canagliflozin senolysis paper is preclinical and is misdated locally as 2025 even though the PubMed record is 2024 ([Nature Aging canagliflozin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39504587/), [henagliflozin biomarker RCT](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40318366/), [GLP-1 aging mouse study](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39801184/), [LDN RCT](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38258677/), [LDN pilot](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23359310/)).  
The evidence source list is also contaminated by interviews, ScienceAlert, XPRIZE, and X/Twitter “community intelligence” ([sources.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/references/sources.md#L175), [sources.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/references/sources.md#L187)). Those can be scouting inputs, not tiering inputs.

5. **Prompt engineering for distillation**  
The current format is not strict enough for clean supervision. Sample outputs already drift into `Evidence tier: A/B` and split confidence into “HIGH for mechanism, MODERATE for human translation,” which is not machine-parseable ([test-mechanistic.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/examples/test-mechanistic.md#L49), [test-mechanistic.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/examples/test-mechanistic.md#L121), [test-mechanistic.md](/Users/biobook/datasets/prism-framework/examples/test-mechanistic.md#L132)). For v3, use a hard schema: `route`, `problem_repr`, `top_hypotheses`, `decisive_findings_for`, `decisive_findings_against`, `final_answer`, `uncertainty`, `next_step`, `diagnostic_confidence`, `clinical_evidence_tier`, `mechanistic_confidence`. Cap rationale length by route, interpret only abnormal or decision-relevant labs, and omit the longevity note unless the route is explicitly preventive. For a small model, concise decision traces will distill better than lush prose CoT.

6. **Tool use integration**  
Yes, v3 should be tool-aware. The highest-value tools are calculators (`anion gap`, corrected sodium/calcium, HOMA-IR, FIB-4, eGFR, MELD), medication safety tools (interaction checker, renal/hepatic dosing, pregnancy/lactation, QT risk), and guideline retrieval (ADA, ACC/AHA, KDIGO, ACOG, IDSA, ATS/ERS, Surviving Sepsis). Add a `Tool-needed?` gate immediately after routing; then insert a compact `Derived values / Retrieved rule` step before convergence. This will let you remove many brittle hard-coded thresholds and replace them with sourceable rules.

7. **Scaling to 2,000-5,000 examples**  
You need a coverage matrix, not a pile of vignettes. Stratify by route, specialty, acuity, age band, pregnancy status, longitudinal vs snapshot data, missing-data/refusal cases, medication-heavy cases, and confounded cases. Weight the dataset toward ordinary clinical reasoning, not longevity: about 60-70% diagnosis/management, 15-20% labs/preventive, 10% acute/emergency, 5-10% basic science/pharmacology. Include benign/no-pathology cases and near-miss distractor pairs, otherwise the model will learn that every vignette hides metabolic syndrome, inflammation, or “subclinical” endocrinology.

8. **Top v3 changes by impact**  
- Split PRISM into route-specific prompts and training subsets. The current one-size-fits-all chain is the main failure mode.  
- Make evidence claim-specific, not drug-specific, and separate `clinical evidence` from `mechanistic confidence`.  
- Demote longevity overlays to an optional preventive module. Acute, pediatric, pregnancy, psych, and geriatric cases should not carry the same hallmarks/optimization burden.  
- Replace universal cascade tracing with a 3-level mechanism budget: physiologic, pathway, molecular.  
- Make lab interpretation abnormal-only and task-conditional. Step 4 currently bloats traces and encourages overmedicalization.  
- Implement real templates for acute care, pregnancy, pediatrics, psychiatry, geriatrics, and post-op instead of just route labels.  
- Add tool support for calculations, dosing/interactions, and guideline retrieval.  
- Ban non-primary/media/social sources from evidence tiering.  
- Enforce a rigid output schema with single-value labels and automated contradiction checks.  
- Consider two-stage distillation: long teacher rationale for generation, compressed student rationale for training.
